Computational Pathology Group

The Computational Pathology group has been active in its current form since 2013, while foundations were made in the early 1980's. We have a strong translational focus, owing to our firm embedding in a clinical environment. We have a leading position in our research field, witnessed for instance by our highly successful CAMELYON16 and CAMELYON17 grand challenges. We aim to remain at the cutting edge of computational pathology by fulfilling an active role in committees and conference organization.

Camelyon16 published in JAMA

The Diagnostic Image Analysis Group organized Camelyon16, the first medical image analysis challenge with whole slide digital pathology images in 2016. The competition was a great success, and several of the submitted software solutions outperformed human pathologists in the detection of lymph node metastases.
